## Resolution and aperture

### Evidence

- Lenstip reports center resolution at F1.2 around 69.7 lpmm. This is already higher than the stopped-down maximum of some older / cheaper Sony 50mm class lenses in the same test context.
- Lenstip-related snippets and later comparison references indicate the lens reaches about 78 lpmm around F2.8 and around 80 lpmm near F4.
- Lenstip reports APS-C edge performance is decent from wide open and improves quickly, with maximum around 66 lpmm.
- Lenstip reports full-frame edge is safely above the decency threshold even at F1.2, and can exceed 63 lpmm stopped down.
- Lenstip's Sony FE 50mm F1.4 GM review reports a higher center peak: 90.9 lpmm at F2.8.
- Lenstip's Sigma 50mm F1.2 DG DN Art review notes stopped-down performance of modern 50mm F1.2 lenses can be very close in real photos, while also naming the Sony FE 50mm F1.4 GM as a 90.9 lpmm record-level lens.
- TechRadar and OpticalLimits summaries agree that the F1.2 GM is already highly usable wide open, with strong center sharpness.

### Interpretation

- F1.2 is genuinely usable, not a soft special-effect aperture.
- The strongest claim for the F1.2 GM is not peak MTF against the F1.4 GM. On Lenstip data, the FE 50mm F1.4 GM has the higher center peak.
- The F1.2 GM's value is "F1.2の表現力を、実用解像とAFで使えること."
- F2-F2.8 is the practical stability zone when F1.2 depth is too thin.
- F4 is likely near the center peak in Lenstip's system; F5.6-F8 are safer for frame-wide work and depth.
- F11-F16 should be used for depth or sunstars, not as default resolution apertures.

### Practical use

- F1.2: portrait, wedding, low light, maximum subject separation. Use when rendering is the reason for carrying this lens.
- F1.4-F2: keep much of the F1.2 look while improving keeper rate for faces and slight movement.
- F2.8-F4: detail, product-ish cuts, high-resolution portraits, safer family / event work.
- F5.6-F8: frame-wide stability, travel / landscape / architecture.
- F11+: depth / light effect only.

## Bokeh

### Evidence

- Sony positions the lens around "dramatic G Master bokeh" at F1.2 and cites an 11-blade circular aperture plus spherical aberration control.
- Lenstip says out-of-focus images are very nice, with smooth light spread; onion-ring bokeh is not strongly felt despite aspherical elements.
- Lenstip notes mechanical vignetting is clearly visible at F1.2, moderate at F1.8, and gone by F2.5.
- Cameralabs / TechRadar / user discussions commonly treat the F1.2 look, separation, and bokeh rendering as the major reason to choose this lens.
- User discussions often describe the F1.2 GM as having a distinct rendering character compared with F1.4 GM, but this is impression-based and should be handled carefully.

### Interpretation

- "Why F1.2 GM?" is primarily about rendering: extra blur, lower-light headroom, and F1.2 subject separation while retaining high practical sharpness.
- It should not be framed as a simple "sharper than F1.4 GM" purchase.
- Mechanical vignetting / cat's-eye behavior is part of the F1.2 look and should be checked for point-light-heavy compositions.
- If the user wants maximum bokeh and a 50mm main lens, this is the more expressive choice than the F1.4 GM.

### Practical use

- Best suited for portraits, weddings, night street, environmental portraits, and subject isolation where F1.2 visibly changes the image.
- For children / moving family work, F1.4-F2 may be the practical sweet spot, because F1.2 depth is extremely thin.
- For background lights, test corner bokeh shape and mechanical vignetting if round highlights are important.

## Aberrations

### Evidence

- Lenstip's CA chapter indicates lateral CA is not a major issue; available snippets and comparison notes describe strong correction overall.
- Lenstip's bokeh chapter notes smooth light spread and limited onion-ring visibility, suggesting controlled spherical aberration behavior in bokeh rendering.
- OpticalLimits summary reports some bokeh fringing / LoCA, while also describing distortion and lateral CA as low.
- TechRadar reports no visible chromatic aberration in practical testing.

### Interpretation

- Do not write "色収差はゼロ." The safer statement is that lateral CA appears well controlled and LoCA / bokeh fringing may still appear in some F1.2 high-contrast cases.
- As an F1.2 lens, it is better corrected than the aperture would suggest, but not exempt from F1.2 physics.
- In UI text, CA should be "良好だがF1.2の高輝度前後ボケは確認" rather than a headline weakness.

### Practical use

- F1.2 portraits: generally usable, but inspect high-contrast eyelashes, jewelry, white clothing edges, and specular highlights if delivering large prints.
- F1.4-F2: likely reduces visible LoCA / focus-plane ambiguity while keeping much of the rendering.

## Vignetting, flare, coma, night lights

### Evidence

- TechRadar and Photography Blog both call out strong / visible vignetting wide open.
- Lenstip vignetting chapter exists and the summary trend aligns with heavy full-frame vignetting at F1.2.
- OpticalLimits summary notes heavy RAW vignetting at F1.2 and help from auto-correction.
- Lenstip coma / bokeh chapter says APS-C-area coma is not bothersome, but full-frame corners at F1.2 show visible point-light problems / wings; stopping down 1 EV reduces them strongly.
- Lenstip reports astigmatism at 17.8%, a medium value close to high.
- Sony claims Nano AR Coating II minimizes internal reflections, but practical reviews should be used before claiming exceptional flare resistance.

### Interpretation

- The major optical caution is wide-open vignetting.
- For portraits, vignetting can be part of the look. For product, reproduction, uniform backgrounds, or sky, correction is expected.
- For night lights, F1.2 is usable, but full-frame corner point lights should be checked. F1.8-F2.5 may clean up mechanical vignetting / point-light shape.
- Do not position this as a dedicated astro lens without additional star-field evidence.

### Practical use

- F1.2: embrace corner falloff for portraits / wedding / night mood.
- F2-F2.8: use if corner brightness and point lights matter.
- F5.6-F8: frame-wide stability.
- For sun-in-frame flare, verify with examples; do not rely only on Sony coating claims.

## Close focus / magnification

### Evidence

- Official specs: minimum focus distance 0.4m.
- Official specs: maximum magnification 0.17x.
- Sony notes floating focus mechanism and high resolution over the focus range.

### Interpretation

- Close focus is useful for detail cutaways, food on table, hands, accessories, wedding details, but not a macro replacement.
- 0.17x is not enough to sell it as a table-photo specialist.
- The value is "F1.2 rendering at normal-to-close distances," not magnification.

### Practical use

- Good: rings, hands, bouquet, small details with environmental context.
- Not ideal: tiny objects, copy work, product catalog work requiring flatness / magnification.

## AF / video

### Evidence

- Sony official press release: four XD Linear Motors; fast, precise, quiet AF and tracking; designed to keep subjects sharp at extremely shallow depth.
- Sony press release mentions 4K video with human eye tracking, minimum delay, low vibration, aperture ring click on/off, Linear Response MF.
- TechRadar and OpticalLimits summaries align on fast / accurate AF and professional build.
- Cameralabs notes focus breathing as a consideration, while still describing filming potential positively.
- OpticalLimits summary says videographers may like de-clickable aperture ring and compatibility with Sony breathing compensation depending on body.

### Interpretation

- Native Sony AF is a major reason to choose this lens over adapted or manual F1.2 options.
- For children / weddings / events, F1.2 depth is the limiting factor more than AF motor performance.
- Video value depends on body support for breathing compensation and tolerance for size / weight.
- Do not claim "breathing no issue" without body-specific verification.

### Practical use

- AF-C / Eye AF at F1.2-F2: useful for portraits and weddings, but keeper rate depends on subject motion.
- Video: strong candidate for controlled / cinematic 50mm shots; verify breathing and balance on rig / gimbal.
- Long handheld sessions: 778g matters.

## Comparison notes

### Sony FE 50mm F1.4 GM

- F1.4 GM is lighter: approx. 516g vs 778g.
- F1.4 GM has smaller filter: 67mm vs 72mm.
- Lenstip reports FE 50mm F1.4 GM center peak at 90.9 lpmm, higher than the F1.2 GM's measured peak in the same general test ecosystem.
- F1.4 GM is the practical sharpness / portability choice.
- F1.2 GM is the rendering / maximum blur / low-light expression choice.
- Lens Navi framing: "F1.4 GMは開放実用性と携帯性、F1.2 GMはF1.2の画作り."

### Sigma 50mm F1.4 DG DN Art

- Sigma 50mm F1.4 DG DN Art is the price / value challenger.
- It should be compared if the user does not specifically need F1.2 rendering or Sony-native premium handling.
- Lens Navi framing: "絞って使う / 価格重視ならSigmaも比較。ただしSony F1.2 GMはF1.2表現と純正AFを買う."

### Sigma 50mm F1.2 DG DN Art

- Not requested as primary comparison, but relevant in 2026 market context.
- Lenstip suggests modern 50mm F1.2 lenses can be very close stopped down; real-world differences should be handled carefully.
- Needs separate research if Lens Navi starts recommending F1.2 alternatives.

### Sony FE 50mm F1.8

- Lightness and price option.
- Not comparable in AF / build / controls / open-aperture rendering.
- Use only as budget / first-50 reference.

## High confidence

- Official specs: approx. 778g, 72mm filter, 0.4m minimum focus, 0.17x maximum magnification.
- AF drive: four XD Linear Motors.
- F1.2 open aperture is genuinely usable in the center; Lenstip reports around 69.7 lpmm at F1.2.
- Lenstip-supported comparison: FE 50mm F1.4 GM has higher peak center resolution, including the 90.9 lpmm value.
- The F1.2 GM's purchase logic is rendering / F1.2 look / subject separation, not peak-resolution superiority over F1.4 GM.
- Wide-open vignetting is a consistent caution across multiple sources.
- Bokeh is a central strength; onion-ring bokeh is not a major complaint in Lenstip's analysis.
- Coma / point-light behavior at full-frame corners needs care at F1.2; stopping down helps.

## Needs verification

- Exact Lenstip CA values for longitudinal / lateral CA should be copied into future UI only after opening each chapter directly or recording values manually.
- OpticalLimits primary page should be checked directly, not only via SonyAddict excerpt.
- Phillip Reeve, Dustin Abbott, asobinet, and long-term user reviews need direct extraction before writing final "攻略本" copy.
- Focus breathing: body-dependent Sony breathing compensation support needs confirmation for target camera bodies.
- Star-field / astro suitability needs dedicated samples; current evidence is coma / point-light oriented, not full astro field testing.
- Whether F1.2 GM has meaningfully "better bokeh quality" than F1.4 GM is partly subjective and should be phrased as rendering preference unless side-by-side sources agree.
- Sigma 50mm F1.2 DG DN Art should be researched separately if used as a current comparison candidate.

## Lens Navi暫定結論

Sony FE 50mm F1.2 GM 最大の強みは、F1.2の大きなボケ量と表現力を、実用的な解像と純正AFで使えること。

F1.4 GMよりピーク解像が高いレンズとして売るべきではない。Lenstip系の測定では、ピーク解像はF1.4 GMの方が上と整理するのが安全。

最大の注意点は、778gの携帯負荷、開放周辺減光、F1.2の浅すぎる被写界深度、そして動画時のブリージング確認。

F1.4 GMとの違いは、実用性能の優劣ではなく、選ぶ価値の違い。F1.4 GMは開放実用性と携帯性、F1.2 GMはF1.2の画作りと表現力を買うレンズ。

まだ完成版レビューにはしない。次に進むなら、OpticalLimits / Dustin Abbott / Phillip Reeve / asobinet から、ボケ・動画・逆光・長期使用の実写傾向を追加確認する。
